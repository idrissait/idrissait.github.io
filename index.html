About

In my current role at Dharma Platform, the organization’s leading Implementation Specialist, I oversee the rollout of digital health technology across health programs in Europe, the Middle East, Africa and Asia working in close partnership with Ministries of Health, NGOs, and UN agencies. In this role I’ve found a unique way to combine my multidisciplinary interest in biostatistics and R, epidemiology, digital technology, GIS and health systems to support public health workers around the world. Since 2017 I’ve supported clinical trials on malnutrition in Niger, helped measure the impact of seasonal malaria chemoprevention in the Sahel, carried out quantitative and spatial analysis in Nigeria, run mortality surveys in DR Congo and mapped water points across the Middle East. A key component of my work has been building the capacity of health workers to embrace digital technology in order to improve case management, strengthen data quality and validity, gather insights faster during public health crises, and make data-informed decisions to save lives and restore dignity.
